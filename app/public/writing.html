"HOLLOW"

I like the feeling of hollow.

The emptiness
In-between the collarbone.

Show me some spine.
Give me some ribs.

Spread your thighs.

Best if they don’t touch.

The gap is supposed
To be
Beautiful—

So I like the feeling of hollow.



"CITIES"

People
Collapsing the streets—
Distorted faces,
Edvard Munch paints
Them against
The reflective skyscrapers.

Although the city
Still breathes,
The minds of millions
Suffocate the streets—
Thought bubbles
Like umbrellas
Dot the boulevard.


Trees
Pumping oxygen into the
Industrial landscape,
The minority provides
A crucial backbone,
Forests and trees web
A network
In-between concrete
And plexiglass.


Each face impassive,
The ability
To separate,
To disengage,
And hide in its turtle shell
Of a skull.

"KALEIDOSCOPE"

A knot
Waiting
For bend,
A bruise
Stalling
For scab,
But nothing less
Than mine, mind
In flight—
When you
Let
Go.
Don’t stop.
Twirl it.
A blonde
Waits for sun,
The flower
Sits for bloom,
Spinning mosaics,
Wilting then transforming
Out of doom.

"WHITER THAN SNOW"

Cry,
Let your eyes
Be cast in white.

Cascade
Like sheets of lace,
Let the flood
In your heart
Become blight,

Seep out through your eyes
So that all
You have
Left
Is obstructed sight,
And your dryness
Will be worth something.

Lose something.
Lose your tears,
Release your fears—
You are wet.
And this wetness
Needs to be offset.

So wash me.
Wash me whiter than snow.

Strip me of my color,
Milk me from my joy,
And let me drip
Slowly
Into myself.

So I am washed white;
You gonna stop
My bright,

But I am whiter
Than snow,
So my light
Transcends
Past the edge
Of your sight.


"WILLOW TREE"

Swaying

Leaves

Drop.

A tree

And a

Trunk

Occupy

Shade,

Light

Dancing

Through

Negative

Space.

Its sway

Shakes the

Influence.


"SHOOTING STAR"

The stars echo;
The sky unveils
To weep.

The black veil
Is lifted
Where the
blurs turn
In the
Resonate lure
Of light,
As the tears still
blind
And the bodies
crisscross
In indecipherable shadow.

Why?

So grey people
can look
At a
Black sky
And wish
On a
White star.

"HIGH TIDE"

The sea’s been cast;
The walls come
Crumbling down
To boast, undulations
Forcing the tide
Across the shore.

The sea twisting
Like a stubborn dancer,
Crushing sand particles
Into entropic atoms,
Spreading the life
Of the sea
Across the concrete,
Fortifying brick,
And rocky crust.

The earth depraved;
The people stargazed,
As they sit jet-lagged,
Staring down at the sea
Surrounding their ankles
And swallowing their toes.

They cry—
We don’t want to get wet,

But they do
And they drown
And the earth drowns with them.
And with no more earth
And no more people,
There is just sea,
Just sea.

"WATER ORNAMENT"

Her skin
Drips
With water.

Adorning her skin,
Slipping lastly
Off her toes,
A crown of thorns
Turns into
A halo.

Shrouded in spring water,
Christened in the cleansing
cascade of an hourglass.

Pure,
Untainted,
She is shrouded
In the
Clear transparency
Of a foreign and
Unfamiliar cloak.
