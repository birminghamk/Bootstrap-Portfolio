The Masters Gallery website needed a face-lift,
not only to enhance the visual appeal,
but to focus all the attention where it should be–

ON THE ARTISTS.

Utilizing a black and white color scheme, I added stylistic and content upgrades, creating a unified, sleek theme that captures the brand, and directs the user's eye to the artists, and their bodies of work.

To further emphasize the artists' work, I adjusted the distribution and alignment of various page elements to remedy the uneven spacing and create visual balance.

In addition to stylistic changes, there was a lack of content spanning major sections of the website, including the 'About Us' and 'Exhibitions' tabs. These sections are dictated by the website's current CMS as opposed to the internal code base, with a blog-like structure.

I added engaging and informative content, highlighting the gallery's stunning interior and decades of experience.

The website makeover was complete and looked spanking new, but it needed to be consistent, regardless of how the user was viewing it; using CSS Media Queries, I made the site responsive across all screen sizes.
